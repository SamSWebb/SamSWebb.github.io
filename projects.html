Pending publications

Automated Scoring of the OCS-Plus Computer-Tablet Figure Copy Test - validation

  • This project introduces and validates a novel automated scoring programme written in python which is able to reliably and accurately score the Figure Copy test from the OCS-Plus with extremely high sensitivity to elements and total scores when compared to two trained human raters
  • Pre-print is available here, accepted in Neuropsychology- awaiting proofs.

The COVID-19 pandemic altered the modality, but not the frequency, of formal cognitive assessment: Standardised and reliable remote cognitive tests are urgently required

  • The project investigated the impacts of the pandemic on rates of cognitive assessments and established whether there was an overall shift to teleneuropsychology.

  • Pre-print is available on PsyArxiv, accepted in Disability and Rehabilitation - awaiting proofs.

Stage 1 Accepted Registered Reports:

Pre-Attentive Processing in Visuospatial Neglect: Burning Houses Revisited

  • The project seeks to replicate the pre-attentive semantic processing effect first documented by Marshall and Halligan (1988) under more stringent experimental conditions, and in a broad, representative sample while investigating whether semantic or merely physical stimuli differences are responsible for modulating the occurrence of pre-attentive processing in neglect patients.

Preprints:

Multiverse Analysis: A Method to Determine Researcher Degrees of Freedom in Test Validation

  • This project builds off my MSc work at the university of Oxford and explores how we can use multiverse to highlight validation outcome fragility (whether we find significant validation comparisons or not) in validation studies using executive functioning tests
  • This project uses data from the OxMET normative project and OCS-Plus project.
  • Pre-print available on PsyArxiv - under revision

Ongoing Projects 2021:

Relation of the OxMET to measures of functional ability in a stroke rehabilitation setting

  • This is my main PhD project, where I am collecting data in a sub-acute stroke rehabilitation setting. The data include the OxMET as well as the Barthel Index and the Therapy Outcomes Measure, modified Rankin Scale and National Institute of Health Stroke Scale.

  • Status: Under active recruitment

Relation of the OxMET to instrumental activities of daily living 6- and 12-months post-stroke

  • This will look longitudinally at the OxMET to establish whether performance on the OxMET in the sub-acute phase of recovery will predict performance on instrumental activities of daily living 6 post-stroke.
  • Status: Under active recruitment

Further validation of the Oxford Cognitive Screen-Plus in Sub-Acute Stroke Rehabilitation Setting

  • This projects aims to provide further construct validity for the OCS-Plus by comparing construct-matched tests to each test from the OCS-Plus.

  • We are using different tests to the original normative validation due to improvements in matching test constructs

  • Moreover completing the validation in a sub-acute stroke sample brings greater variance to the data with less floor and ceiling effects expected, leading to greater power in our analyses.

  • Status: Under active recruitment

The Oxford COMPetency ASSessment(COMPASS): A Brief Supplemental Cognitive Assessment Aligned with Mental Capacity Criteria

  • This project validates a tool intended as a brief supplementary assessment of someones cognition related to the ability to make decisions as defined in law.
  • The OxMET task is within the COMPASS but has a separate paper due to the standalone ability of the OxMET to assess cognition. The tasks included in the COMPASS assess comprehension, retention, and weighing up.
  • in analysis phase.
